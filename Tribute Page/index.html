Nikola Tesla

The Man Who Invented the 20th Century

A Timeline of His Life & Achievements

  • 1856 - Born in Smiljan, Austrian Empire (modern-day Croatia).
  • 1882 - Began working for the Continental Edison Company in Paris, where he conceived the idea for the induction motor.
  • 1884 - Immigrated to the United States and briefly worked with Thomas Edison before they parted ways.
  • 1887 - Founded the Tesla Electric Company and filed for several patents for his AC induction motor and polyphase system.
  • 1891 - Invented the "Tesla Coil," an electrical resonant transformer circuit used to produce high-voltage, low-current, high-frequency alternating-current electricity.
  • 1893 - Demonstrated wireless lighting and power transmission at the World's Columbian Exposition in Chicago.
  • 1898 - Demonstrated the world's first radio-controlled boat, showcasing the potential of wireless communication and robotics.
  • 1899 - Conducted experiments in Colorado Springs, studying atmospheric electricity and achieving groundbreaking results in high-voltage power transmission.
  • 1901-1917 - Worked on the Wardenclyffe Tower project, an early wireless transmission station designed for transatlantic wireless telephony and broadcasting.
  • 1943 - Died at the age of 86 in New York City. Much of his work laid the foundation for modern electrical and communication systems.
